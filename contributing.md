# Contribution Guidelines

Thank you for helping make this list better! Please ensure your pull request adheres to the following guidelines.

## Adding a paper

- Search previous entries before adding a new one to avoid duplicates.
- One paper or resource per pull request. This makes review and discussion easier.
- Place the entry in the correct year section and the most fitting subsection, at the bottom of that subsection.
- Use the entry format: `- [Title](link) - First author et al., venue or year. Optional one-line note. ` followed by tags in backticks.
- The description starts with an uppercase character and ends with a period.
- Link to the canonical source: the arXiv abstract page, DOI, official blog post, or model card. No paywalled mirrors or link shorteners.
- Add landmark papers with a short "why it matters" note. Keep it factual, not promotional.
- Check your spelling and grammar, and make sure the link works.

## What belongs here

- Research on small language models: pre-training, fine-tuning, PEFT, distillation, quantization, inference, on-device deployment, evaluation, and safety.
- Foundational work that made SLMs possible, even if it predates the term.
- Work on low-resource and Indic languages where small models are the practical deployment path.

## What does not belong here

- Papers about large models with no relevance to efficiency, compression, or small-model training.
- Unmaintained tools, archived repos, or resources without documentation.
- Marketing pages, paid courses, or content that primarily promotes a product.

## Updating or removing an entry

If a link breaks, a paper is withdrawn, or a resource becomes unmaintained, open an issue or a pull request explaining the change.

Thank you for your contribution!
